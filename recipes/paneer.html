Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a popular North Indian vegetarian dish featuring soft paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a rich and creamy tomato-based sauce, infused with aromatic spices and finished with a touch of butter.

Paneer Butter Masala, also known as Paneer Makhani, is a delectable vegetarian delight that has become a staple in Indian cuisine. To prepare this mouthwatering dish, cubes of paneer are lightly fried until golden, enhancing their texture and flavor. In a separate pan, a luscious sauce is created by sautéing onions, garlic, ginger, and a blend of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves, followed by the addition of ripe tomatoes, creating a rich and tangy base

The sauce is then blended to a smooth consistency and returned to the pan, where cream and a dollop of butter are added, infusing the dish with velvety indulgence. The fried paneer cubes are gently folded into the sauce, allowing them to absorb the flavors, resulting in a harmonious and creamy symphony of tastes. Garnished with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro, Paneer Butter Masala is best enjoyed with naan, roti, or fragrant basmati rice, making it a truly irresistible and comforting dish loved by vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ pound paneer, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon ground cashews
  • 1 teaspoon ground red chiles
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ½ cup half-and-half
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Steps:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat; fry paneer in batches until golden, about 5 minutes. Transfer fried paneer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain, retaining vegetable oil in skillet.
  2. Melt butter in the same skillet over medium heat; cook and stir onion until golden brown, about 10 minutes. Add ginger paste and garlic paste. Continue to cook until fragrant, about 1 minute more. Stir cashews, ground red chiles, cumin, coriander, and garam masala into the onion mixture. Cook and stir for 1 minute.
  3. Stir tomato sauce, half-and-half, milk, sugar, and salt into spice mixture; simmer until thickened, about 5 minutes. Reduce heat to low. Add fried paneer and simmer until heated through, about 5 minutes more.
